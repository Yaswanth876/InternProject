🌪️ Disaster Preparedness Squad

India faces many climatic disasters like cyclones, floods, and heat waves. What causes these disasters? How can we protect ourselves and our communities? This question helps you explore real-life examples and apply what you learn to make better decisions during emergencies.

📗 Reference

Grade 7 Social Science – Chapter 3: Climates of India
Topic: Climatic Disasters (Cyclones, Floods, Landslides, Fires)

🕵‍♀ Scenario: Disaster Preparedness Squad

You join a school club teaching villagers about cyclones, floods, and heat waves.
You role-play a cyclone hitting the coast, then practice evacuation.
You design posters showing how topography and wind cause disasters.
You simulate flood scenarios using simple models.
You present emergency steps to build community resilience.

🧠 Logic Used

Inference and risk assessment (linking factors to disaster outcomes).

💡 Hint

Remember how low pressure over warm seas spawns cyclones.

🧮 Algorithm

  1. Identify factors (wind, topography, rainfall) for each disaster.
  2. Link each factor to the cause (e.g., heavy rain → floods).
  3. Assess impacts on people, crops, and infrastructure.
  4. Propose preventive measures or evacuation plans.

🧩 Think and Decide

  • Which factor mainly causes landslides in the Himalayas?
    Answer: Heavy rain and deforestation on steep slopes.
  • How do urban heat islands worsen heat waves?
    Answer: Concrete traps heat, raising city temperatures further.
  • What role can community drills play before a cyclone?
    Answer: They build quick evacuation skills, saving lives.

✅ Let’s Think!

  1. Should we restrict building in floodplains even if the land is scarce?
    Hint: Consider long-term safety vs. short-term need.
  2. How might forest conservation reduce disaster risk?
    Hint: Trees bind soil, reduce runoff, and absorb heat.
  3. Is it ethical to relocate entire villages from high-risk zones?
    Hint: Weigh individual rights against collective safety.
